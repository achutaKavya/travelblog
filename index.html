The Taj Mahal - Agra

This iconic white marble mausoleum is one of the most famous landmarks in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal and is renowned for its stunning architecture and intricate details.

Jaipur - Rajasthan

Known as the Pink City, Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan and is famous for its historic palaces, forts, and vibrant markets. The City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amer Fort are some of the top attractions in Jaipur.

Kerala Backwaters

The backwaters of Kerala offer a unique and serene experience. You can take a houseboat cruise through these tranquil waterways, surrounded by lush green landscapes, coconut trees, and traditional villages.

Goa

Known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and a mix of Indian and Portuguese culture, Goa is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. Besides the beaches, you can explore historic churches and enjoy water sports.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi, in Uttar Pradesh, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, renowned as the spiritual heart of India, situated along the sacred Ganges River. It's famous for its historic ghats, temples, and the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti ceremonies, attracting pilgrims and travelers alike seeking to cleanse their sins and immerse themselves in India's rich spirituality and heritage.

Rishikesh and Haridwar

hese two cities are spiritual and adventure hubs, located along the banks of the holy Ganges River. Rishikesh is famous for yoga and adventure sports, while Haridwar is known for its religious significance and the Ganga Aarti on the Har Ki Pauri ghat.
